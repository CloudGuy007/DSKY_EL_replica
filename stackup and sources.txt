Display stackup:

1.  ITO-coated glass (1.1mm thick) :  https://www.ebay.com/itm/401338724836
(clean thoroughly in acetone and then IPA)

2.  Screen-printed silver epoxy dots (originally provided the common electrode connection, but just decorative here).  This could be replaced by lots of other things.
		https://www.amazon.com/MG-Chemicals-Two-Part-Conductive-Adhesive/dp/B003BDMJSY

3.  Screen-printed black epoxy lettering ("NOUN", "VERB", etc)
		https://www.mclogan.com/shop/nazdar-ade-series-epoxy-screen-ink-p-1145.html
		ADE-52 Opaque black

4.  Sprayed layer of Lumilor green phosphor mixed with a dash of black pigment
		https://www.coastairbrush.com/proddetail.asp?prod=Lumilor_LumiColor_4oz&cat=386
		https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000AP6OHI

5.  Sprayed layer of Lumilor dielectric
		https://www.coastairbrush.com/proddetail.asp?prod=Lumilor_Dielectric_4oz&cat=386

6.  Screen printed conductive paint to define display segments
		https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007R9UMXM

7.  3M 9703 electrically conductive adhesive transfer tape (anisotropically conductive, or "Z tape")
		https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00HDDR19U

8.  DSKY_SEG  PCB with copper traces to match segment positions
		Currently, the common electrode (ITO) is connected to the PCB by scratching away a tiny bit of the paint at the border
		and using magnet wire and conductive epoxy to connect it electrically.
